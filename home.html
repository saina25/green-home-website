What is Renewable Energy?

Renewable energy is energy derived from natural sources that are replenished at a higher rate than they are consumed. Sunlight and wind, for example, are such sources that are constantly being replenished. Renewable energy sources are plentiful and all around us.

Fossil fuels - coal, oil and gas - on the other hand, are non-renewable resources that take hundreds of millions of years to form. Fossil fuels, when burned to produce energy, cause harmful greenhouse gas emissions, such as carbon dioxide.

Generating renewable energy creates far lower emissions than burning fossil fuels. Transitioning from fossil fuels, which currently account for the lion’s share of emissions, to renewable energy is key to addressing the climate crisis.

Renewables are now cheaper in most countries, and generate three times more jobs than fossil fuels.

What are our Renewable Resources?

Solar Energy

Solar energy is the most abundant of all energy resources and can even be harnessed in cloudy weather. The rate at which solar energy is intercepted by the Earth is about 10,000 times greater than the rate at which humankind consumes energy.

Wind Energy

Wind energy is a clean, domestic renewable energy source and is one of the fastest-growing energy sources in the world. Besides helping diversify the nation’s energy portfolio, it provides many economic benefits to communities.

Hydroelectric Energy

Hydroelectric power is one of the oldest and largest sources of renewable energy, which uses the natural flow of moving water to generate electricity. It is a domestic source of energy, allowing each state to produce its own energy independently.

Ocean Energy

Ocean energy systems are still at an early stage of development, with a number of prototype wave and tidal current devices being explored. The theoretical potential for ocean energy easily exceeds present human energy requirements.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy utilizes the accessible thermal energy from the Earth’s interior. Heat is extracted from geothermal reservoirs using wells or other means. Once at the surface, fluids of various temperatures can be used to generate electricity.

Bio Energy

Bioenergy is produced from a variety of organic materials, called biomass, such as wood, charcoal, dung and other manures for heat and power production, and agricultural crops for liquid biofuels

Why Green Home?

Fossil fuels, such as oil, coal, and natural gas, on the contrary, are available in finite quantities only. This is because, although they are produced through natural processes, they do not replenish as quickly as we humans use them. Not only are fossil fuels bound to run out sooner or later, but what’s worse is that their extraction and production is associated with climate-damaging greenhouse gases, as well as health-endangering particles.

Making the home energy-efficient before installing a renewable energy system will save money on electricity bills. Energy-efficiency improvements can conserve energy and prevent heat or cool air from escaping. Homeowners can obtain home energy assessments and install proper insulation, air sealing, and ENERGY STAR®–qualified windows, heating and cooling equipment, kitchen appliances, and lighting systems.
